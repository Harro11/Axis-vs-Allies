Game Overview

Axis vs Allies is a classic strategy board game that simulates the epic struggle of World War II. Players take control of the major powers of the war, making strategic decisions about military production, troop movements, and combat to achieve victory for their side.

The game balances historical accuracy with engaging gameplay, offering both casual and serious players a chance to rewrite or relive the history of this global conflict. Will the Axis powers achieve world domination, or will the Allies prevail in defending freedom?

Factions

Players command the major world powers embroiled in World War II, each with unique starting positions, resources, and strategic advantages.

Axis Powers
Germany Japan Italy

The Axis powers begin the game with significant territorial control in Europe and Asia but face enemies on multiple fronts. They must coordinate their strategies to prevent being overwhelmed.

Strengths:

  • Powerful initial land armies
  • Strong defensive positions
  • Technologically advanced units

Strategies:

  • Quick expansion to secure additional resources
  • Prevent Allied cooperation by controlling key sea zones
  • Develop overwhelming force on one front before pivoting to another
Allied Powers
United States United Kingdom Soviet Union

The Allied powers must defend their territories while building up their industrial capacity. They typically start with greater industrial potential but are strategically disadvantaged.

Strengths:

  • Superior industrial production capacity
  • Stronger naval forces
  • Ability to coordinate multi-front operations

Strategies:

  • Protect industrial centers to maximize production
  • Use naval superiority to control supply lines
  • Gradually increase pressure on multiple fronts

Game Resources

Resources and industrial capacity play a crucial role in the game strategy.

Industrial Production Credits (IPCs)

The primary currency in Axis vs Allies. Used to purchase new military units and develop technologies.

Each territory contributes a specific IPC value to the controlling nation's economy each turn.

High-value territories include:

  • Eastern United States (20 IPCs)
  • Western United States (10 IPCs)
  • United Kingdom (8 IPCs)
  • Germany (15 IPCs)
  • Russia (10 IPCs)
  • Japan (10 IPCs)
  • India (6 IPCs)

Industrial Complexes

Facilities that allow the production of new military units in a territory.

Strategic targets that should be both protected and targeted during the game.

Starting industrial complexes are located in:

  • Eastern United States
  • Western United States
  • United Kingdom
  • Germany
  • Russia
  • Japan

Strategic Territories

Territories with high IPC values or strategic location are critical for success.

Chokepoints and important regions include:

  • Egypt - Gateway between Africa and Middle East
  • Alaska - Connection between Asia and North America
  • India - High-value Asian territory
  • Western Europe - Key battleground
  • Siberia - Buffer zone between Russia and Japan
  • South Africa - Strategic naval positioning

Game Phases

Axis vs Allies is played in sequential turns, with each nation completing all phases before the next nation's turn.

1
Purchase Units

During this phase, the active player uses IPCs to purchase new military units and technologies.

  • Units can only be placed in territories with industrial complexes.
  • The number of units produced cannot exceed the IPC value of the territory with the industrial complex.
  • Purchased units are set aside until the Place New Units phase.
  • Strategic planning is crucial - decide between many cheap units or fewer powerful ones.
2
Combat Movement

Move units into territories or sea zones where you intend to initiate combat.

  • Units can move up to their movement allowance.
  • Land units cannot move through enemy territories.
  • Aircraft have extended range but must land in friendly territory or on a carrier.
  • Strategic choices include concentrated attacks or multi-front assaults.
3
Combat Resolution

Resolve battles one by one in any order the attacking player chooses.

  • Each combat round consists of an attacker roll followed by a defender roll.
  • Units score hits when their roll is less than or equal to their attack/defense value.
  • The attacker decides which defending units are eliminated, then the defender does the same for attacking units.
  • Combat continues until one side is eliminated or the attacker decides to retreat.
4
Non-Combat Movement

Move remaining units that did not participate in combat or retreated.

  • Reinforce vulnerable territories.
  • Reposition aircraft that participated in combat.
  • Consolidate forces for future operations.
  • Move ships to establish defensive lines or prepare for next turn's assaults.
5
Place New Units

Place units purchased at the beginning of the turn.

  • New land and air units must be placed in territories containing industrial complexes.
  • New naval units must be placed in sea zones adjacent to territories containing industrial complexes.
  • Placed units cannot participate in combat until your next turn.
6
Collect Income

Collect IPCs based on the total value of territories you control.

  • Each territory has a specific IPC value that represents its economic worth.
  • Your national production includes all territories under your control.
  • This income will be used for purchases in your next turn.
  • Strategic bombing raids by enemies can reduce the amount of income collected.

How To Play Axis vs Allies

Game Setup

Before starting the game, you'll need to set up the board according to the historical starting positions of World War II.

1. Distribute Units

Place the starting units for each nation on their corresponding territories as indicated in the setup chart.

2. Distribute IPCs

Give each player their starting IPCs based on the total value of their controlled territories.

The turn order is: Soviet Union, Germany, United Kingdom, Japan, United States.
